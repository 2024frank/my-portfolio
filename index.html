About Me

FRANK KUSI APPIAH

My name is Frank Kusi Appiah, and I am from Ghana. From an early age, I have been fascinated by technology and how it shapes our world. My journey began when I was just seven years old and received my first iPad. I was amazed by how the software worked and how images and people appeared on the TV screen. At that age, I even believed the people were shrunk and placed inside the TV! That sense of wonder and curiosity laid the foundation for my passion for computer science and electrical engineering.

I grew up in Brofuyedru, a small suburb in the Ashanti region of Ghana. Living in a community with limited resources taught me how to be innovative and creative with what I had. I found joy in solving problems, especially ones that could help improve the lives of people around me. Whether it was tinkering with gadgets or trying to understand how things worked, I was constantly driven to learn more about technology.

Over time, my interest in technology has expanded into creating solutions that merge both computer science and electrical engineering. I have worked on projects that focus on real-world applications, emphasizing innovation, efficiency, and accessibility. My background has taught me resilience, adaptability, and the ability to think critically about how to use technology to overcome challenges.

I am passionate about using my skills to make a difference, especially in underprivileged communities like the one I grew up in. My goal is to continue learning, exploring, and contributing to the ever-evolving field of technology while inspiring others from similar backgrounds to believe in the power of innovation.
